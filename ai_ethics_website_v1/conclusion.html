Conclusion

AI can advance medicine, education, accessibility and economic growth. Yet without responsible design and governance it can also concentrate power, magnify bias, or erode privacy and accountability. Ethical AI is therefore not a single rule but an ongoing practice: align systems to human values, measure impacts, involve stakeholders, and improve continuously. When developers, organisations and regulators collaborate, AI can remain trustworthy while delivering real benefits to society.
