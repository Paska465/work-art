Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to computational systems that perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence, such as recognising patterns, making predictions, or generating content. As AI spreads through finance, health, education, transport and entertainment, the ethical implications become central to how societies protect rights and promote the common good.

Ethical AI aims to ensure systems are beneficial and do not cause undue harm. Core themes include fairness (avoiding unjust discrimination), transparency (explaining how systems work and make decisions), accountability (clear responsibility for outcomes), privacy (respecting personal data and consent), safety and security (resisting misuse and failures), and sustainability (considering social and environmental impacts).

Good AI is not only about technical accuracy; it is about aligning technology with human values, legal rights, and social purpose.
